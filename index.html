What is Domain Authority and Page Authority?

Domain authority is a logarithmic scale developed by Moz to tell search engine ranking score and likeness of a website to get ranked. Moreover, as the name explains, it refers to the authority of a domain and signals the authoritative strength of the overall domain on a scale of 0 to 100.

There are 40 different factors that contribute to evaluating the Moz domain authority like linking root domains, the total number of backlinks, and the quality of websites linking to it. After early 2019, Moz updated its algorithm and now calculating the DA of a website on machine learning algorithms that foresee how many times that specific domain is appearing in Google search results.

The Industry leader in the world of SEO Sarah from Web Wizard points out, "DA is a score that shows your website's readiness to compete online. A high DA means smoother sailing towards search engine supremacy."

On the other side, page authority refers to the authority of a specific page or number of pages instead of the overall domain. It again shows the likeliness of a specific page getting ranked on search engines. It is also developed by Moz where the scale ranges from 1 to 100. Based on specific page performance, you can see how well your page is performing with the competitors.

PA or page authority is also calculated by Moz AI machine learning algorithm and takes into account different factors like traffic linked to the specific page, inbound/outbound links linked to a page, keyword density, and even page experience. Moz PA scale tells the rankability strength of a page in comparison to others in the same domain or of competitors.

Industry leader Emily from SEO Expertise highlights, "PA focuses on how well your website's individual pages can perform. It's the key to unlocking an effective content strategy – strong pages mean a powerful website."

What is the Importance of DA PA in SEO?

Domain authority and page authority provide valuable insights into how well a website is performing on search engine result pages. This is why both of these logarithmic scales are important when it comes to determining if a website or web page is well-optimized and well-reputed or not.

In simple words, DA and PA mark the ranking strength of a website and web page, respectively. You must know that whenever we analyze a website or web page from the SEO perspective, we take a special look at how much ranking strength it has. This is because ranking strength helps us to decide if the website or web page is worth linking out or buying or not.

It is important to note that apparently page authority is not considered a ranking factor but it has a huge impact on website domain authority. The more pages of a website have a higher authority, the more domain authority it gains. Whereas the better the domain authority score, the more quickly it ranks and becomes reputable.

To be more clear, the DA PA significantly helps to earn the trust of search engines as well as users. More trust means more authority whereas more authority means more ranking strength.

Backlink building comes first to mind when we talk about the DA PA of a website. This is because a quality backlink significantly boosts the authority of a website or web page.

When you pay special attention to identifying and meeting the needs of your users, your website automatically starts to gain more ranking strength.

For example, you have a fashion blog. Perform market research and figure out what your target audience likes to read about the most. Prioritize Keyword Relevance

If you want your website or web pages to gain more authority then focus on keyword relevance. Always target the right and relevant keywords in your content.

The search engine is more likely to rank the content that's completely relevant to the user's search query. Well, this doesn't mean that if you randomly stuff your content with relevant keywords then it will rank higher.

You need to understand that the keywords must be strategically chosen and then used in the content to yield good results.

For instance, you are writing an article about "digital marketing" for your website. It would be best if you add relevant keywords like "digital marketing definition" "digital marketing importance", "digital marketing benefits", and "digital marketing fields" in your content.
